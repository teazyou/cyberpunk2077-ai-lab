## About this mod

All of my Named NPCs Mods in one. High-resolution 4K or 2K retexture of 42 different NPCs from the base game.

 Permissions and credits  Changelogs  Donations 

**High-resolution textures of 42 Named NPCs from the base game. Includes faces, hair, cyberware, eyes, clothing, jewelry & accessories, and other unique aspects of each NPC. 

For details on specific NPCS, visit the individual mod pages.**

I have upscaled all textures by 4x using[chaiNNer](https://github.com/chaiNNer-org)and[ESRGAN models](https://upscale.wiki/wiki/Model_Database), to a maximum of 4K.

A 2K option is available if you're concerned about your VRAM.
Phantom Liberty NPCs are not included. When I've completed those individually then I'll make an AIO for those as well.

Checkout my[Minor NPCs Mod](https://www.nexusmods.com/cyberpunk2077/mods/8045)for complete coverage.

**Installation**

This is a bundle of 42 different archive files in one zip. Thanks to v2 they are now setup in a FOMOD installer to make it easy to choose which NPCs you want to install. For manual installation choose the optional files.
Cyberpunk loads archives in alpha numerical order, with numbers before letters. Archive names that come first get loaded last, meaning they will overwrite anything that comes after them.

I have added a prefix of"zz" at the beginning of the mod archive names so that it will be overwritten by other mods. If instead you want an archive to overwrite another mod then change the prefix to "00" at the beginning of the mod archive name.

Yorinobu Arasaka**So what's with all these High-Res Texture Mods?**

Cyberpunk relies heavily on post-processing and native upscaling for it's graphics. That means that most of the vanilla textures are very low resolution, with the game engine upscaling most of them at least 2x but as much as 32x the original, depending on your graphics settings.

That technique allows CDPR to package the game much smaller, but the quality of this on-the-fly upscaling is nowhere near what carefully trained and chosen upscale models can do with powerful AI software.Since vanilla textures are already being upscaled, there shouldn't be much of a difference in VRAM usage between these mods and putting your Texture Quality setting to High.

The biggest improvements are increased definition. CP77 relies heavily on normal maps for it's textures and then applying colors and effects to those normal maps. AI-upscaled normal maps greatly increase the definition of the detailing, allowing light and shadow to fall better.
